4. Suggested wording improvements (copy you can paste)
Below are small refinements, not rewrites.

❓ “What happens if I miss a check-in?”
Current

We will remind you. If you still don’t respond, we will notify your emergency contacts.

Improved

We’ll remind you first.
If you still don’t respond after the grace period, we’ll gently notify your emergency contacts so they can check on you.

Why better:


“gently” matters


“grace period” reassures


“check on you” feels human



❓ “What if my phone is off?”
Current

If we can't reach you, your contacts may be notified when your check-in is missed.

Improved

If we can’t reach you, and a check-in is missed, your emergency contacts may be notified so they know to check in on you.

Removes ambiguity and keeps tone supportive.

❓ “Will you share my location?”
Current

Only if you turn location sharing on. You can turn it off anytime.

Improved

Only if you turn location sharing on.
You’re always in control and can turn it off anytime.

Adds a control signal (very important for trust).

❓ “What happens when a contact responds?”
Current

If a contact says "I'm handling this", we pause further alerts while they check on you.

Improved

If a contact responds with “I’m handling this,” we pause further alerts while they check on you, so you’re not overwhelmed.

Shows care for the user’s experience, not just logic.

❓ “What is the grace period?”
Current

After your check-in is due, we wait for the grace period (10–30 minutes) before notifying your contacts.

Improved

After your check-in is due, we wait 10–30 minutes before notifying your contacts.
This gives you time to respond if you’re just running late.

Very reassuring. Keep this.

❓ “Can I pause check-ins temporarily?”
Current

Yes! In Settings, you can pause alerts for 2 hours, 6 hours, or until tomorrow morning.

Improved

Yes. In Settings, you can pause alerts for 2 hours, 6 hours, or until tomorrow morning — perfect for travel, rest, or busy days.

Humanizes usage.
